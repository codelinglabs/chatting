# Chatting

Chatting is an async website chat product for SaaS teams that want to understand what is blocking conversion.

Instead of sending visitors into a long form, demo funnel, or support queue, Chatting gives them a simple way to ask a question directly on the site. Those questions land in a team inbox, where someone can review the thread, reply, and learn from the pattern behind the message.

## What Chatting Is

Chatting sits between live chat and a contact form.

It is not built around always-on support agents or real-time handoff. It is built for teams that want a lightweight, conversational way to capture high-intent questions and turn them into useful product and revenue insight.

At its core, Chatting combines three things:

- An embeddable chat widget for your site
- A workspace inbox for your team
- A simple system for continuing conversations through chat and optional email

## Why It Exists

A lot of visitors leave a site with unanswered questions.

They may be confused about pricing, unsure whether the product fits their use case, blocked by a bug, or simply missing one piece of information that would have helped them sign up. Most of those people never open a support ticket or book a sales call.

Chatting exists to catch those moments.

It gives teams a direct line to the questions people ask right before they leave, and it turns those questions into something actionable instead of letting them disappear.

## Who It Is For

Chatting is for small SaaS teams, founder-led products, and operators who want to stay close to user questions without installing a heavyweight support platform.

It is especially useful for teams that:

- care about conversion and pre-sales friction
- want to hear objections in customers’ own words
- need a simple inbox rather than a full support suite
- want one widget across one or more sites or products

## What the Product Does

From a user’s point of view, Chatting gives a visitor a chat box on the website where they can ask a question without creating an account.

From the team’s point of view, Chatting turns those messages into structured conversations inside a dashboard. The team can read the thread, reply from the dashboard, add tags, and capture feedback on whether the reply was helpful.

If a visitor shares an email address, replies can also be delivered by email. If they do not, the conversation can still continue in the widget itself.

## What Makes Chatting Different

Chatting is not trying to be a full customer support system.

Its value is in being lightweight, focused, and conversion-oriented.

The product is designed around a few principles:

- Website questions are product signals, not just support requests
- Teams should be able to reply without complex setup
- Email should be optional, not required to start a conversation
- The inbox should help teams learn, not just answer

That makes Chatting a good fit for teams that want direct insight from real visitor questions, but do not want the overhead of a larger helpdesk platform.

## Current Product Shape

Today, Chatting includes:

- a customizable website widget
- a team inbox for reading and replying to conversations
- chat-thread replies that appear back in the widget
- optional email continuity
- helpful / not helpful feedback capture
- tags and lightweight conversation stats
- a simple presence signal showing whether the team is active in the dashboard

## The Value in One Sentence

Chatting helps SaaS teams talk to visitors before they leave, reply from one place, and turn every question into insight about what is blocking conversion.
